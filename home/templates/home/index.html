“We are all in the gutter, but some of us are looking at the stars.”

Jeremy was born in 1948 and grew up in Stanstead before attending boarding school at the beautiful Heath Mount in Woodhall Park. He then went on to be educated at Marlborough College, where he met Nick Drake, and also learned about the meaning of life – girls, Chinese porcelain, Oscar Wilde and the south of France. It was the latter where Nick and Jeremy spent months attempting to learn the language.

In 1968 Jeremy joined the Christie’s Oriental Department before leaving to be an independent dealer in 1973. In the meantime, he married Joanna and had two children, Toby and Becky. In 1999, due to a brain aneurysm, Jo went blind literally overnight, but thanks to her unwavering strength, has continued to be an inspiration to Jeremy and anyone else who meets her.

For over 50 years, sometimes in a shop in Ebury Street, he has whiled away the time dealing, painting, collecting, and reading, punctuated by 3 months a year in the South of France, which he had fallen in love with many years before.

They have lived in a house in Wandsworth built in 1710 that is now filled with Jeremy’s extensive collection of Oscar Wilde ephemera and a swathe of unconsidered trifles – a giant cabinet of curiosities.
